## Full Version

**Subject:** Collaboration Inquiry -- Computational MTHFR Structural Analysis and Experimental Validation

Dear Dr. [Last Name],

I am reaching out to explore potential collaboration on MTHFR polymorphisms, specifically C677T and A1298C.

Your published work on [insert specific paper, topic, or finding] stood out to me because of its relevance to MTHFR biology and variant-driven functional effects. Building on that area, I completed a computational structural analysis designed to generate experimentally testable hypotheses for validation.

Using AlphaFold 3 and Boltz-2, I compared wild-type MTHFR and variant states in the presence of FAD cofactor. In the tested model configurations, the main signal was preserved monomer folding with comparative differences emerging at the dimer and interface level. In particular, the compound heterozygous model showed lower interaction-confidence values at the dimer interface relative to wild-type and single-variant comparators.

This work does not claim mechanism, disease causation, or therapeutic benefit. Its purpose is to prioritize bounded experimental questions for wet-lab follow-up. The current hypotheses focus on dimer-level biology, biochemical readouts relevant to one-carbon metabolism, and downstream indications such as retinal and neuropsychiatric phenotypes.

I am also a compound heterozygous carrier of C677T and A1298C, which is part of why I pursued this project, but the collaboration request is grounded in the computational results and the experimental questions they raise.

I have prepared the following materials:

- A full research document with literature-supported rationale
- Structural comparison outputs and confidence metrics
- A replication workflow for the modeling steps
- A GitHub repository with sequences, protocols, and analysis materials

Project repository: https://github.com/DSMPromo/mthfr-target-validation

I am seeking a research partner with wet-lab capabilities to validate the structural predictions and assess whether the hypotheses merit further experimental development. If this is of interest, I would value a brief 15-minute conversation.
